## Surge Watch

[Attention Residuals](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.15031v1) from the Kimi team remains the dominant paper in this cycle — 183 HF upvotes, 3.2k GitHub stars, and 8 citations (3 influential). Growth has plateaued over the past two weeks, but the absolute numbers are hard to ignore for an attention mechanism paper.

[TriAttention](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.04921v1) had the most explosive trajectory: 24→112 HF upvotes and 9→670 GitHub stars in under a month. KV compression via trigonometric encoding clearly struck a nerve. Traction is now leveling off, but 2 citations already suggest academic pickup.

[Mamba-3](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.15569v1) is the academic sleeper — 16 citations with only 6 HF upvotes. Researchers are quietly building on SSM principles while the community looks elsewhere. Worth watching for a second wave if Mamba-based models start shipping.

[Accelerating Speculative Decoding with Block Diffusion Draft Trees](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.12989v1) tells a developer-first story: 310 GitHub stars but just 6 HF upvotes. People aren't upvoting — they're cloning the repo.

[FlashAttention-4](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05451v1) continues accumulating citations (now 9, 1 influential) with essentially zero community visibility (1 HF upvote). Academic infrastructure work that will matter more when Blackwell ships widely.

Broader signal: the initial burst from March/April papers has cooled across the board. Nearly every tracked paper shows flat signals over the past 5–7 days. The next wave of momentum likely depends on new releases rather than continued traction on existing work.
